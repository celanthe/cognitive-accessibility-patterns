# Ritual-Based Transitions

> A repeatable structure for processing what has no resolution.

## Cognitive Mechanism

Grief, ambiguous loss, and emotional overwhelm resist linear processing. The brain cannot "solve" the loss of someone who is still alive but gone, or the slow erosion of a role that defined you. Traditional software either ignores these states or tries to fix them (journaling prompts, mood trackers, CBT exercises). Ritual offers a different mechanism: a repeatable, bounded container that does not demand resolution. The ritual holds the experience without requiring the user to narrate, explain, or improve.

Ritual works because it is predictable. The brain can enter a known structure during high emotional load without spending executive function on deciding what to do. The ritual decides. The user just shows up.

## The Pattern

Provide a short, structured, repeatable interaction that:
- Has a clear beginning and end (the user knows when it starts and when it is done)
- Requires minimal decision-making (the ritual provides the structure)
- Does not demand verbal processing (no journaling, no prompts, no "how are you feeling?")
- Uses sensory or environmental grounding (weather, time of day, ambient data)
- Distinguishes between degrees of difficulty (a hard day is not the same as a catastrophe)
- Never tracks streaks, frequency, or completion

The ritual is an offer. Skipping it is a valid interaction. Silence is a valid response.

## Evidence

- **Foghorn**: Uses daily weather as the ritual anchor. The user checks in with their local weather conditions, which Foghorn maps to emotional texture. Fog means something different than clear sky. The ritual is the same structure every day, but the content shifts with conditions. There are no journaling prompts. No five stages. No progress tracking. Just a daily moment that acknowledges ambiguous loss without trying to resolve it.

## COGA Mapping

- **COGA Task Force gap (2018)**: The COGA task force flagged grief and emotional regulation as gaps in cognitive accessibility guidance. No success criteria or design patterns were developed. Ritual-based transitions are a direct response to that gap.
- **Objective 6: Minimize the user's cognitive load** -- The ritual's predictable structure eliminates decision overhead during high emotional load.

## Not This

- **Mood tracking** -- Mood trackers ask the user to label and quantify their emotional state. Ritual-based transitions do not ask for labels. The ritual holds the space without requiring categorization.
- **Journaling prompts** -- Journaling demands verbal processing. Ritual-based transitions deliberately avoid requiring the user to produce language about their experience.
- **Meditation apps** -- Meditation is a practice with its own goals (mindfulness, calm, focus). Ritual-based transitions are not trying to achieve a state. They are containers for an experience that already exists.
- **Habit tracking** -- Ritual-based transitions must never track streaks or frequency. Showing up on day 1 after a 30-day gap is exactly as valid as day 31 of consecutive use.
